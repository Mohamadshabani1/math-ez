What is a quadratic equation?

A quadratic equation is a quadratic trinomial equation consisting of only one variable

The quadratic equation:

ax2+bx+c=0

The number of roots (solutions) of the quadratic equation reaches up to two solutions as its degree is 2, but how can we know the number of solutions in the quadratic equation?

The discriminant is used to find the number of solutions in a quadratic equation

The discriminant:

b2-4ac

If the result is greater than one, there are two real solutions (intersecting with the horizontal axis(x) at two points)

If the result is zero, there is only one solution (one intersection with the horizontal axis)

If the result is less than zero, there will be no real roots, and the equation is going to have 2 complex roots.



How can we find the answers?

We use the quadratic formula to know the answer/s

The quadratic formula:

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]

When a=0, there is one solution to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b + \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]

When a<0 , there is 2 unreal solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]
